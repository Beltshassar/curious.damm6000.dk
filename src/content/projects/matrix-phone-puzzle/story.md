In our family we do a lot of geocaching. My brother also makes caches for others to find. We had an idea for a movie themed cache with multiple puzzles. One of the steps will be a phone call with a secret message in the middle of a forest.

I gutted an old landline phone and fitted an Arduino inside. Now, you can connect a USB power bank, and the phone starts ringing. If the handset is lifted, a sound sequence from The Matrix is played, ending with a strange and garbled sound. When analyzed in a spectrogram app, the sound waves will draw a secret message, with a GPS coordinate showing directions to the next puzzle.

In early testing, my teenage daughter was deeply fascinated, especially with the tactile interaction and the fact that audible noise can carry a simple image.
