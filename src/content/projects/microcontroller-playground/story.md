I am volunteering in a local church club. A few years ago, a fellow volunteer and I came up with the idea of playing around with electronics at one of the club nights.

I had a stack of ESP8266 microcontrollers and thought we could have some fun with those. I brought microcontrollers, a bunch of jumpers, breadboards, LEDs, speakers and switches, and to make the whole thing accessible for the 4th-6th graders, I found a website able to convert Scratch-like block-coding into ESP-ready code, then I made a few example setups and let the kids loose!

We had a lot of fun. At first they were mostly following the recipes, but as soon as they got the basics, quite a few started modding the examples, expanding on what they had just learned.
